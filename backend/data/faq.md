# SleepPilot FAQ

## 1. What is SleepPilot?
SleepPilot is a sleep optimization app that helps users improve their sleep habits through personalized recommendations, sleep tracking, and routine planning. It analyzes sleep logs, wearable data, and lifestyle inputs to suggest better bedtime routines, wake-up times, and sleep environment adjustments.

## 2. How does SleepPilot improve my sleep?
SleepPilot looks at patterns in your sleep schedule, sleep duration, wake-up consistency, screen time, caffeine timing, and relaxation habits. Based on these patterns, it gives practical suggestions such as adjusting your bedtime, creating a wind-down routine, reducing late caffeine intake, or improving sleep consistency.

## 3. Does SleepPilot diagnose sleep disorders?
No. SleepPilot is not a medical device and does not diagnose, treat, or cure sleep disorders. It is designed for sleep habit improvement and wellness guidance. If you have symptoms such as chronic insomnia, loud snoring, breathing pauses during sleep, or extreme daytime sleepiness, you should consult a healthcare professional.

## 4. What data does SleepPilot collect?
SleepPilot may collect sleep logs, bedtime and wake-up time, sleep quality ratings, lifestyle inputs such as caffeine or exercise timing, and optional wearable data if you connect a supported device. SleepPilot does not require users to share private messages, browsing history, or unrelated personal files.

## 5. Does SleepPilot work without a wearable device?
Yes. SleepPilot can work without a wearable device. Users can manually enter their bedtime, wake-up time, sleep quality, and daily habits. Wearables can improve convenience and provide additional sleep signals, but they are not required to use the app.

## 6. Which wearable devices does SleepPilot support?
SleepPilot is designed to support common wearable platforms such as Apple Health, Google Fit, Fitbit, and Garmin through available integrations. Actual support may depend on the user’s device, operating system, and permission settings.

## 7. How does SleepPilot protect my privacy?
SleepPilot is privacy-first. It only uses sleep-related data that users choose to enter or connect. Personal sleep insights are shown to the user, and SleepPilot does not sell personal sleep data to advertisers. Users can disconnect integrations and delete their data from the app settings.

## 8. Can SleepPilot help with jet lag?
Yes. SleepPilot can help users prepare for travel by suggesting gradual bedtime and wake-up adjustments before a trip. It can also recommend light exposure timing, sleep schedule shifts, and routine changes to help the body adapt to a new time zone.

## 9. What is the SleepPilot sleep score?
The SleepPilot sleep score is a simple summary of sleep consistency, duration, self-reported quality, and routine stability. It is meant to help users notice trends over time, not to judge their health. A low score should be treated as a signal to review habits, not as a medical diagnosis.

## 10. Does SleepPilot have a smart alarm?
Yes. SleepPilot includes a smart alarm feature that can recommend a wake-up window based on the user’s preferred wake-up time and recent sleep pattern. The goal is to help users wake up at a more suitable time while still respecting their schedule.

## 11. Can SleepPilot create a bedtime routine for me?
Yes. SleepPilot can create a personalized bedtime routine based on your target wake-up time, current sleep habits, and lifestyle inputs. A routine may include reducing screen time, setting a relaxation period, preparing the room environment, and setting a consistent sleep schedule.

## 12. Is SleepPilot suitable for students?
Yes. SleepPilot is suitable for students who want to manage sleep around school, exams, studying, and irregular schedules. It can help students plan consistent sleep routines, avoid very late caffeine use, and build healthier sleep habits during stressful periods.

## 13. Can SleepPilot help if I sleep too late every night?
Yes. SleepPilot can suggest gradual schedule adjustments instead of forcing a sudden large change. For example, it may recommend moving bedtime earlier by 15 to 30 minutes every few nights, reducing late-night screen exposure, and setting a consistent wake-up time.

## 14. How much does SleepPilot cost?
SleepPilot offers a free plan with basic sleep logging, sleep score tracking, and general recommendations. A premium plan adds advanced trend analysis, smart alarm features, wearable integrations, travel support, and deeper personalized routine planning.

## 15. What should I do if SleepPilot cannot answer my question?
If SleepPilot cannot answer your question from its FAQ or product information, it should say that it does not have enough information. The assistant should only answer questions about SleepPilot, sleep tracking features, privacy, pricing, integrations, and sleep routine guidance.
